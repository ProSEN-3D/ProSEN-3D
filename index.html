Abstract

High-fidelity 3D asset generation from text or images is increasingly important in fields such as industrial design and gaming. Early 2D-guided methods suffer from distortions due to the lack of 3D priors, while recent 3D-native models improve fidelity but remain limited by the scarcity and cost of high-quality 3D training data. To address this, we propose \textbf{ProSEN-3D}, a progressive self-enhancement framework that improves pretrained 3D-native generative models without relying on additional human-curated data. Our key insight is that high-quality outputs already exist sparsely within the modelâ€™s output space and can be repurposed to drive further improvement. To efficiently discover such outputs, we introduce a quality-driven exploration strategy that broadens the sampling space via diversity-guided inference and refines selection through pivot recursive search. To fully exploit these high-quality samples, we develop a two-stage self-regularized refinement pipeline: a preference-based contrastive learning guides the model toward better outputs, followed by fine-tuning on top-ranked generations to consolidate improvements. Together, these components form a closed loop of exploration and refinement,n explore-refine closed loop that enables continual self-improvement while preserving generalization. Experiments on the Toys4K benchmark demonstrate that ProSEN-3D significantly outperforms prior methods, achieving stronger prompt alignment and higher visual fidelity.
